## The Switzerland Mortgage Market

The Swiss National Bank sets the benchmark interest rate influencing mortgage pricing in Switzerland. Lenders offer fixed, variable, and hybrid products. Use our [mortgage calculator](/calculator/?country=ch) to compare repayment scenarios.

## How Much Can You Borrow?

Lenders in Switzerland assess borrowing capacity based on income, existing debts, employment stability, and property value. Most lenders use a debt-to-income ratio to cap total borrowing. Read our [first home buyer guide](/switzerland/blog/first-home-buyer-guide/) for eligibility details.

## Types of Mortgages

Switzerland lenders offer fixed rate mortgages (rate locked for a set term), variable rate mortgages (move with benchmark rates), and hybrid products. Our [mortgage types guide](/switzerland/blog/mortgage-types-compared/) explains the pros and cons of each.

## The Application Process

Gather proof of income, bank statements, identification, and property details before applying. Pre-approval strengthens your buying position in Switzerland. Most mortgage approvals take 2-6 weeks from full application.

## Costs of Getting a Mortgage

Budget for valuation fees, lender arrangement fees, mortgage registration costs, and legal fees — typically 1-3% added to the cost of the loan. See our [buying costs guide](/switzerland/blog/buying-costs-explained/).

## Refinancing Your Mortgage

Review your rate at the end of each fixed term. Switching lenders or products can save significantly over the life of your loan in Switzerland. Our [refinancing guide](/switzerland/blog/refinancing-guide/) covers when and how to switch.
