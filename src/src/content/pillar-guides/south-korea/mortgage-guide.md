## How Mortgages Work in South Korea

A mortgage is a loan secured against property. The Bank of Korea sets benchmark rates influencing lender pricing. Use our [mortgage calculator](/calculator/?country=kr) to see how rates affect repayments.

## Types of Mortgages Available

Fixed rate locks in your rate for a set period. Variable rate moves with the market. Read our [mortgage types guide](/south-korea/blog/mortgage-types-compared/) for a detailed comparison.

## The Application Process

Get pre-approved first, then apply formally once you find a property. You will need proof of income, ID, bank statements, and details of existing debts.

## Understanding Interest Rates

The Bank of Korea influences rates through monetary policy. Always compare total loan cost including fees, not just the headline rate.

## Managing Your Mortgage

Extra repayments reduce total interest significantly. Consider refinancing if better rates become available — see our [refinancing guide](/south-korea/blog/refinancing-guide/).
