The buyer typically pays the notaio's fees, which usually range from €1,500 to €3,000 depending on the property value."

Most Italian property transactions use the caparra confirmatoria for stronger legal protection."

It offers complete certainty about future payments, making budgeting straightforward and protecting borrowers from Euribor or ECB rate increases. Fixed rates in Italy are indexed to the IRS (Interest Rate Swap), so they tend to be higher than initial variable rates but provide long-term security."

  - question: "What is the spread in an Italian mortgage and how does it affect my rate?"
    answer: "The spread is the bank's fixed margin added on top of the benchmark rate (Euribor for variable, IRS for fixed) to arrive at your total mortgage interest rate. For example, if the 20-year IRS is 2.5% and the bank's spread is 1.0%, your fixed rate is 3.5%. Banks set the spread based on your risk profile, the LTV ratio, and market competition, so a stronger financial profile can help you negotiate a lower spread."

Italian law requires banks to disclose the TAEG prominently, making it the most accurate tool for comparing the true cost of mortgages from different lenders. Always compare TAEG figures, not just the nominal interest rate."

  - question: "What is the difference between a mutuo fondiario and a mutuo ipotecario in Italy?"
    answer: "A mutuo fondiario is a specific type of long-term mortgage governed by the Testo Unico Bancario (articles 38–42) where the loan cannot exceed 80% of the property's value and must have a duration between 18 months and 30 years, offering borrowers stronger legal protections including limits on the bank's ability to call in the loan early. A mutuo ipotecario is a broader category of mortgage secured by a property mortgage (ipoteca) that does not need to meet those specific TUB thresholds, and is often used for higher loan-to-value ratios or shorter terms. In practice most Italian home purchase loans are structured as mutui fondiari because the regulatory framework benefits both banks (preferential treatment in insolvency) and borrowers (capped early repayment penalties on variable-rate loans). Understanding which type your bank is offering is important when comparing costs and protections at the proposta di mutuo stage."

    answer: "When buying a new-build property directly from a developer (costruttore) and the property qualifies as your prima casa, the VAT (IVA) applied to the purchase price is 4% instead of the standard 10% or luxury rate of 22%, representing a significant saving on higher-value purchases. To benefit from the 4% rate you must meet the standard prima casa requirements: the property must be in the municipality where you live or work, it must not be classified as a luxury property under the decreto ministeriale 2 agosto 1969 (categories A/1, A/8, A/9), and you must not already own another prima casa in Italy. Unlike resale purchases where the imposta di registro applies, new-build sales from a developer within four years of completion are subject to IVA, and the notaio will verify eligibility at the rogito. If you lose the prima casa qualification within five years, the tax authority will recover the difference between the 4% paid and the full applicable rate plus interest and penalties."
