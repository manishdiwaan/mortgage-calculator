- question: "What is the FEIN document in a Spanish mortgage?"
  answer: "The FEIN (Ficha Europea de Información Normalizada) is a standardized European mortgage information sheet that lenders must provide to borrowers in Spain under the 2019 Mortgage Law. It details all key loan terms including interest rate, APR, monthly payments, total cost, and associated risks. The FEIN is legally binding for ten business days, meaning the lender cannot change the offered terms during this period, giving you time to compare offers."

While the FEIN summarizes the mortgage terms, the FIAE specifically highlights the risks of the mortgage product, such as the impact of Euribor fluctuations on variable-rate loans or early repayment charges. Both documents must be provided at least ten business days before signing, and your notary will confirm you have received and understood them."

Your variable mortgage rate is typically expressed as Euribor plus a fixed spread, for example Euribor + 0.99%. Most Spanish variable mortgages are reviewed annually or semi-annually, meaning your monthly payment adjusts when Euribor changes. In recent years Euribor rose sharply from negative levels to above 4%, significantly increasing payments for variable-rate borrowers."

  answer: "Spanish variable-rate mortgages (hipotecas variables) are typically indexed to the 12-month Euribor, with the interest rate reviewed and reset either annually or semi-annually based on the Euribor value published on a specific reference date stipulated in the mortgage deed. After Euribor peaked above 4% in late 2023, monthly payments on many variable mortgages increased by €200 to €400 compared to 2021 levels, demonstrating the significant payment volatility these products carry. If you prefer payment certainty, a fixed or mixed-rate mortgage avoids this annual uncertainty entirely."
