  - question: "How much down payment do I need to buy a house in Singapore?"
    answer: "For HDB flats with HDB loan: 10% (can use CPF). For bank loans on HDB: 25% down (5% cash, 20% CPF or cash). For private property: 25% down (5% cash compulsory, 20% CPF or cash) for first property. Second property requires 25% (minimum 25% cash) plus ABSD. Loan-to-value limits are set by MAS and HDB and may change with property cooling measures."

  - question: "What is a good mortgage rate in Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore mortgage rates are among the most competitive in Asia. Fixed rates (1-5 years) and floating rates (SORA-linked or board rates) are both widely available. SORA (Singapore Overnight Rate Average) replaced SIBOR as the benchmark for floating rate loans. Compare rates across multiple banks — rates can vary by 0.3-0.5% for the same borrower profile. Use our mortgage calculator to compare scenarios."

  - question: "How much can I borrow for a mortgage in Singapore?"
    answer: "MAS caps the Total Debt Servicing Ratio (TDSR) at 55% of gross monthly income across all debt obligations. For HDB flats, the Mortgage Servicing Ratio (MSR) further caps housing loan repayments at 30% of gross monthly income. Maximum loan tenure is 25 years for HDB flats (with bank loans) and 30 years for private property. Loans must be repaid by age 65 (bank) or 65 (HDB loan)."

  - question: "What is the difference between fixed and floating rate mortgages in Singapore?"
    answer: "Fixed rates lock your interest for 1-5 years — popular for payment certainty in rising rate environments. Floating rates (SORA + spread, or board rate) move with market conditions and typically start lower but carry rate risk. After fixed period ends, loans revert to floating or a higher board rate. Most buyers choose 2-3 year fixed packages with competitive lock-in rates."

  - question: "Should I use a mortgage broker in Singapore?"
    answer: "Mortgage brokers in Singapore compare products across multiple banks at no charge to you — they earn referral fees from banks. They are particularly useful for complex situations, refinancing, or when buying high-value properties where rate differences matter significantly. Popular platforms include MoneySmart, PropertyGuru Finance, and Redbrick Mortgage Advisory. Always compare at least 3 banks."

  - question: "Can I make extra payments on my Singapore mortgage?"
    answer: "Most Singapore bank mortgages allow partial prepayments during the lock-in period (typically 1-3% penalty applies). After the lock-in, prepayments are usually free. Full early redemption during lock-in attracts the clawback penalty (1.5-2%). Strategy: make additional repayments right after the lock-in expires. CPF OA funds used for mortgage must be returned with accrued interest upon property sale."

  - question: "What documents do I need for a mortgage application in Singapore?"
    answer: "Required documents include NRIC or passport, 3 months payslips, CPF contribution history (via CPF portal), 3 months bank statements, latest Notice of Assessment from IRAS, and property option or sale agreement. Self-employed applicants need 2 years' NOA and business financials. Foreign buyers need additional documents — work pass, employment letter, overseas income proof."

  - question: "What is CPF and how can I use it for my property purchase?"
    answer: "CPF (Central Provident Fund) Ordinary Account (OA) funds can be used for HDB flat purchases (down payment and monthly loan repayments) and private property purchases (down payment and servicing bank loans). The CPF Withdrawal Limit and Valuation Limit apply. Note: CPF funds used must be returned with 2.5% accrued interest when the property is sold — this affects net profit on resale."

  - question: "What is the HDB loan versus bank loan decision?"
    answer: "HDB concessionary loan: fixed 2.6% (0.1% above CPF OA rate), 10% down payment (can be full CPF), no lock-in period, more flexible for first-time buyers. Bank loan: typically lower rates initially but variable, 25% down payment (min 5% cash), lock-in period applies. HDB loan suits first-time buyers with limited cash savings. Bank loan suits those who can afford higher down payment and want lower rates."
    category: "financing"
  - question: "What is a bridging loan in Singapore?"
    answer: "A bridging loan provides short-term financing to bridge the gap between buying a new property and selling an existing one. It covers the down payment on the new property before sale proceeds arrive. Interest rates are higher (typically 5-7% per annum) and loans are typically for 6 months. Most Singapore banks offer bridging loans. Avoid if possible — plan your timeline to minimise need for bridging."

  - question: "What is the MSR (Mortgage Servicing Ratio) and how does it affect HDB buyers?"
    answer: "MSR limits HDB flat loan repayments to 30% of gross monthly income — stricter than TDSR's 55%. For a household earning SGD 8,000/month, MSR caps housing repayments at SGD 2,400/month. This significantly limits borrowing capacity for HDB buyers. EC (Executive Condominium) purchases are also subject to MSR during the initial years. Private property is only subject to TDSR, not MSR."
    category: "financing"
  - question: "What is SORA and how does it affect my mortgage rate?"
    answer: "SORA (Singapore Overnight Rate Average) is the benchmark rate used for floating rate mortgages since SIBOR was phased out. It reflects overnight interbank lending rates and is published daily by MAS. Your mortgage rate is SORA + a bank spread (e.g., 1-month SORA + 0.8%). SORA is generally more stable and transparent than the previous SIBOR/SOR rates. Rate moves broadly track global interest rate trends."

  - question: "How do I buy a property in Singapore?"
    answer: "The process differs by property type. For HDB: check eligibility, apply for HDB flat (BTO or resale), get HDB loan eligibility or bank pre-approval, exercise Option to Purchase (OTP), complete resale application at HDB portal. For private property: get bank pre-approval, grant OTP, exercise within 14-21 days, complete within 8-12 weeks. Foreign buyers may only purchase private property without restrictions."

    answer: "Resale HDB flat: typically 8-10 weeks from OTP to completion. New BTO HDB flat: 3-5 years construction wait from successful ballot. Private resale: 8-12 weeks from OTP to completion. New launch private: typically 1-3 years construction plus 3-6 months administrative processing. Progressive payment scheme applies to new private property — you pay in stages as construction milestones are reached."

  - question: "Do I need a lawyer to buy property in Singapore?"
    answer: "Yes, a conveyancing lawyer is required for all Singapore property transactions to handle the transfer, title search, stamp duty payment, and CPF withdrawal (if applicable). For HDB resale, HDB provides an e-conveyancing portal but buyers can still engage private lawyers. Legal fees for private property typically run SGD 2,000-4,000. HDB resale legal fees are SGD 500-1,500 through HDB's panel law firms."

  - question: "What should I check before buying a property in Singapore?"
    answer: "For HDB: check the Ethnic Integration Policy and Singapore Permanent Resident quota for the block, outstanding HDB loan and upgrades, proximity to MRT and amenities. For private property: check URA Master Plan for nearby development, developer track record, maintenance fee history, lease term (99-year or freehold), and existing tenancies. Commission a professional inspection before exercising OTP."

  - question: "Can foreigners buy property in Singapore?"
    answer: "Foreigners can buy private condominium units, executive condominiums (after 10-year MOP), and landed property on Sentosa Island (with SLA approval). Foreigners cannot buy HDB flats or most landed property without government approval. Singapore Permanent Residents can buy resale HDB flats (subject to quotas) and private property. ABSD of 60% applies to foreigners buying residential property — making Singapore private property very expensive for non-residents."

  - question: "What is the difference between freehold and 99-year leasehold property in Singapore?"
    answer: "Freehold property is owned in perpetuity — rare in Singapore and commands a premium. 99-year leasehold (from government grant date, not your purchase date) is the most common. As the lease runs down below 60 years, financing becomes harder and resale value is affected. 999-year leasehold is effectively freehold. Check the remaining lease tenure — banks may not lend on properties with less than 20 years remaining."

  - question: "What is the Minimum Occupation Period (MOP) for HDB flats?"
    answer: "HDB flat owners must occupy the flat for at least 5 years (MOP) before they can sell it on the open market, rent it out entirely, buy a private property in Singapore, or use it for Airbnb. During MOP, you can rent out spare bedrooms with HDB approval. After MOP, PR owners can buy one private property. MOP starts from the date you collect keys."
    category: "buying-process"
  - question: "What is an Option to Purchase (OTP) in Singapore?"
    answer: "An OTP is a legal document granting the buyer the exclusive right to purchase a property within a set period (21 days for HDB resale, 14 days for private) upon payment of an option fee (1% for HDB, 1-5% for private). If you exercise the OTP by paying the option exercise fee (4-9%), a legally binding contract is formed. If you do not exercise, you forfeit the option fee."

  - question: "What is stamp duty in Singapore?"
    answer: "Buyers pay Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD): 1-4% on first SGD 1.5 million (reaching 6% on amounts above SGD 3 million). Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD) applies on top: Singapore citizens buying 2nd property pay 20%, 3rd+ property 30%. PRs buying 1st property pay 5%, 2nd 30%. Foreigners pay 60%. Married couples with at least one SC buying first home together pay only BSD."

For private property, budget for initial maintenance fees. New launches may require booking fees, stamp duty, and progressive payments."

Inspectors check structural integrity, water seepage, electrical systems, and plumbing. Particularly important for older HDB flats and older condos. New launch buyers should conduct snagging inspection at handover — developers are obligated to rectify defects within the Defects Liability Period."

For private condo: maintenance fees (SGD 200-800+/month depending on facilities and unit size), property tax (4-16% of Annual Value for owner-occupied, 10-20% for investment). Building insurance is included in maintenance fees; contents insurance is separate. Annual property tax bills arrive in December."

  - question: "What government grants are available for first home buyers in Singapore?"
    answer: "HDB provides Enhanced CPF Housing Grant (EHG) of up to SGD 80,000 for first-time buyers based on income. Family Grant (up to SGD 50,000) and Proximity Housing Grant (up to SGD 30,000 for living near parents) are available for resale HDB flat purchases. These grants are credited to your CPF OA and used to reduce the outstanding loan or upfront payment."

  - question: "What is property tax in Singapore and how is it calculated?"
    answer: "Property tax is assessed on the Annual Value (AV) — the estimated annual rental income of the property. Owner-occupied residential property is taxed at 0-16% on a progressive scale. Non-owner-occupied (investment) residential property is taxed at 10-20%. Check your property's AV on IRAS myTax Portal. Tax bills are sent annually in January and can be paid via GIRO, PayNow, or cash."

  - question: "What are maintenance fees for condominiums in Singapore?"
    answer: "Condo maintenance fees cover common area maintenance, security, utilities for common areas, pool and gym maintenance, and sinking fund. Fees range from SGD 200-400/month for mid-tier condos to SGD 500-1,000+/month for large units in luxury developments. Fees increase with unit size and the development's facilities. Check management corporation (MCST) meeting minutes before buying to identify planned special levies."

  - question: "Is property a good investment in Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore private property has been among the best-performing asset classes over the past 25 years, supported by constrained land supply, strong fundamentals, and a stable legal system. However, ABSD cooling measures have significantly raised the cost of investment properties. Gross yields of 2.5-3.5% for private property are relatively low globally — total return (yield plus appreciation) tells a better story over long holding periods."

Core Central Region yields are lower (2-2.5%) while Outside Central Region and suburban condos yield 3-4%. HDB flat rental yields can be 3.5-5% but are subject to restrictions and MOP. Net yields after maintenance fees, property tax, and agent fees are approximately 0.5-1% lower."

  - question: "How is capital gains tax calculated on property in Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore has no capital gains tax on property. However, the Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) applies if you sell within 3 years of purchase: 12% in year 1, 8% in year 2, 4% in year 3, and nil after 3 years. This effectively discourages short-term property speculation. For investment properties, rental income is taxable as ordinary income under IRAS personal income tax rules."

For eligible Singaporean couples buying their first HDB flat with grants, buying almost always wins financially. For private property purchases by PRs or foreigners, the high ABSD means renting may be more economical unless you plan a long (10+ year) stay."

  - question: "What credit score do I need for a mortgage in Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore does not use a single public credit score system. Banks access the Credit Bureau Singapore (CBS) report, which scores borrowers from AA to HH. Banks look at your credit history, existing loans, and income stability. Missed payments, defaults, or too many credit applications can hurt your creditworthiness. Check your CBS report at creditbureau.com.sg before applying."

    answer: "Floating rate mortgages linked to SORA move with Singapore interbank rates, which track global central bank decisions. Fixed rate mortgages protect you during the fixed period (1-5 years). Singapore's interest rate environment is influenced by the US Federal Reserve due to MAS's exchange rate-based monetary policy. Budget for rate increases when assessing affordability — always stress test at 4-5% rates."

However, predicting government intervention is difficult. HDB BTO ballots open periodically and new launch private projects release units quarterly. For resale property, market activity slows over Chinese New Year. Personal financial readiness and life stage matter more than market timing."

  - question: "What is the Ethnic Integration Policy (EIP) and how does it affect HDB resale purchases?"
    answer: "The EIP maintains racial harmony by setting limits on the proportion of each ethnic group in each HDB block and neighbourhood. When buying a resale HDB flat, check that the EIP quota for your ethnicity in the block has not been reached — if it has, you cannot purchase that flat regardless of the seller's agreement. Check EIP status on the HDB resale portal before making any offer."

  - question: "What is an Executive Condominium (EC) and how is it different from a private condo?"
    answer: "ECs are a public-private hybrid — built by private developers but subsidised and with eligibility criteria similar to HDB flats. They are cheaper than comparable private condos and eligible for CPF Housing Grants. ECs have a 5-year MOP, become fully privatised at 10 years (saleable to foreigners), and can then be bought by PRs and foreigners (with ABSD). EC units typically offer larger sizes and better value per sqft."

  - question: "What is the ABSD remission for married couples in Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore Citizen (SC) married couples buying their first home together pay only BSD, no ABSD — but both must be first-time buyers and must both be on the title. If one partner already owns property, ABSD applies. A SC-PR couple pays 5% ABSD. There is an ABSD remission for SC couples selling their existing property within 6 months of buying a new one — the 20% ABSD on the second purchase is refunded."

    answer: "The Urban Redevelopment Authority (URA) Master Plan is Singapore's blueprint for land use over 10-15 years, showing planned zoning changes, MRT extensions, commercial hubs, and green spaces. Before buying, check if there are plans for highways, industrial areas, or tall buildings near your property that might affect views or liveability. URA Master Plan is free to access at ura.gov.sg."

  - question: "What is the Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) and how does it affect property investors?"
    answer: "SSD is charged if you sell residential property within 3 years of purchase: 12% (within 1 year), 8% (within 2 years), 4% (within 3 years). It effectively prevents quick-flip property speculation. Investors must plan for a minimum 3-year hold to avoid SSD. SSD does not apply to industrial or commercial property. Factor SSD into your exit strategy before buying."

  - question: "What is the difference between a new launch and resale private property in Singapore?"
    answer: "New launch properties are sold directly by developers — typically at a premium price but with progressive payment scheme (pay in stages as construction completes), choice of unit, and sometimes waived stamp duty. Resale properties are immediately available for occupation, have established surroundings, and may offer value but require full payment upfront. New launches carry construction risk; resale carries immediate vacancy or tenancy situations."

  - question: "What is the Total Debt Servicing Ratio (TDSR) in Singapore?"
    answer: "TDSR limits your total monthly debt obligations (all loans including mortgage, car loans, credit cards at 5% of outstanding balance) to 55% of gross monthly income. Introduced by MAS in 2013, it prevents over-borrowing. Guarantors are excluded unless they are co-borrowers. Self-employed income is discounted by 30% in TDSR calculations. TDSR is applied at point of loan application — changes to your income or debts can affect refinancing eligibility."

  - question: "What is a HDB BTO (Build-to-Order) flat and how do I apply?"
    answer: "BTO flats are new HDB flats built on demand in a specific location — you apply during a sales launch and wait 3-5 years for construction to complete. Pricing is subsidised below market. Eligibility requires Singapore Citizen household, income ceiling (SGD 14,000 for 4-room and above), no existing property ownership. Balloting is by priority — first-timer families get the most priority. Results are available online within days of ballot exercise."
    category: "buying-process"
  - question: "What is a resale HDB flat levy?"
    answer: "If you received a CPF Housing Grant for your first subsidised HDB flat and later sell it to buy another subsidised flat, you may need to pay a Resale Levy of SGD 15,000-55,000 depending on flat type sold. It is designed to keep subsidies equitable. The levy reduces the subsidy on your second subsidised flat. Buying private property does not trigger the levy — only subsequent subsidised HDB flat purchases."

  - question: "What is the CPF Accrued Interest and how does it affect property sale proceeds?"
    answer: "When you use CPF funds for property (down payment and loan repayments), you must return these funds plus accrued interest (2.5% per annum) to your CPF OA when you sell. This can significantly reduce your cash profit — for example, if you used SGD 200,000 CPF over 10 years, you may need to return SGD 256,000. Always model CPF accrued interest impact before buying to understand your actual profit on resale."

  - question: "What is an en bloc sale in Singapore?"
    answer: "An en bloc (collective sale) occurs when owners of a strata development vote to sell the entire development to a single buyer or developer, typically for redevelopment. Requires 80% consent by share value and strata area (90% for developments under 10 years old). All owners must sell at the agreed price — minority holdouts cannot block a legally approved en bloc. If successful, all owners receive a premium over individual unit values."

  - question: "How does the HDB resale portal work for flat purchases?"
    answer: "The HDB Resale Portal at hdb.gov.sg/resale handles the entire HDB resale transaction online. Buyer and seller register intent to buy/sell, grant and exercise OTP (via portal), submit resale application together, and receive HDB approval within 8 weeks. Both parties can use HDB-approved solicitors or handle directly. The portal eliminated much of the paper-based conveyancing that previously applied to HDB resale transactions."

  - question: "What is the private residential property market classification in Singapore?"
    answer: "URA classifies private residential areas into Core Central Region (CCR: prime Districts 9, 10, 11, and Sentosa), Rest of Central Region (RCR: fringe central areas), and Outside Central Region (OCR: suburban areas). CCR has the highest prices but slowest capital growth recently; OCR offers better value and higher yields. Mass market OCR condos (SGD 1,500-2,000/sqft) versus prime CCR condos (SGD 2,500-4,000+/sqft)."

  - question: "What is HDB income ceiling and how does it affect eligibility?"
    answer: "HDB flats have income ceilings to ensure they remain affordable for middle and lower income households. As of 2024: 2-room Flexi (SGD 7,000 for shorter lease), 3-room (SGD 7,000), 4-room and above (SGD 14,000), and EC up to SGD 16,000). Average monthly household income is assessed. If your income exceeds the ceiling, you must buy private property. Income ceilings are reviewed periodically by HDB."

  - question: "What is a progressive payment scheme for new launch private property?"
    answer: "New launch private property is paid in stages as construction milestones are reached: 5-10% booking fee, followed by payments tied to foundation, concrete framework, brick walls, ceiling, and handover (each milestone triggers a payment tranche). This Normal Progressive Payment Scheme spreads the financial burden over construction. Deferred payment schemes (pay bulk upon completion) are occasionally available but typically at a price premium."

  - question: "What is the ABSD trust structure and has it been closed?"
    answer: "Some buyers previously used trust structures to purchase property for minor children, avoiding ABSD by not having the children own other properties. In 2022, MAS closed this loophole — any residential property held in trust now attracts the highest applicable ABSD rate upfront (35%). The ABSD is refundable only if all trustees are Singapore Citizens buying their first residential property. Legal advice is essential before any trust property structure."
