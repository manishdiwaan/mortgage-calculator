- question: "What is capital gains tax on property in South Africa?"
  answer: "Capital gains tax (CGT) is triggered when you sell a property for more than you paid for it, with the gain calculated as the selling price minus the base cost including purchase price and improvement costs. Individuals have an annual exclusion of R40,000 and pay CGT on 40% of the net gain at their marginal tax rate, resulting in an effective maximum rate of 18%. - question: "What does a lease agreement in South Africa need to include under the Rental Housing Act?"

- question: "What is the eviction process for landlords in South Africa?"
  answer: "Evictions in South Africa are governed by the Prevention of Illegal Eviction from and Unlawful Occupation of Land Act, commonly known as PIE, which strictly prohibits self-help evictions such as changing locks or removing belongings without a court order. A landlord must first cancel the lease and give proper notice, then apply to the Magistrate's Court or High Court for an eviction order, serving the application on the tenant and the municipality. The court considers whether the tenant has alternative accommodation available and may delay the eviction if children or elderly persons are involved. The process typically takes between two and six months even in straightforward cases, highlighting the importance of thorough tenant screening before signing a lease."
