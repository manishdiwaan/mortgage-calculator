Choosing the right mortgage type in United Kingdom can save you tens of thousands over the life of your loan. Here is how the main options compare.

## Fixed Rate Mortgages

Fixed rates lock in your repayments for a set period. You get certainty but less flexibility. Best if you prefer predictable budgeting.

## Variable Rate Mortgages

Variable rates move with market conditions, influenced by the Bank of England's benchmark rate. More flexible, but repayments can increase if rates rise.

## Split Loans

Split loans combine fixed and variable portions, giving you partial certainty with some flexibility. A practical middle ground for many borrowers.

## Interest-Only Loans

Interest-only loans have lower initial repayments but you are not reducing the principal. Primarily used by investors for tax and cash flow reasons.

## Which Type Suits You?

Your choice depends on your risk tolerance, plans, and financial situation. A mortgage broker can help you compare options across multiple lenders.
